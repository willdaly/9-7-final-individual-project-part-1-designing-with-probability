 9.7 Final Individual Project – Part 1: Designing With Probability

### Overview

In this multi-part final project, you will gradually build the conceptual framework for a simple AI-powered study assistant, a system designed to help students study more effectively using the mathematical tools you have learned throughout this course. The final version will be completed in Module 15, with milestone tasks due in modules 9, 11 and 13.

In this first stage, you will apply core ideas from probability and Bayes’ Theorem to model how your assistant might make intelligent decisions or recommendations under uncertainty.

###  Instructions

#### Assignment brief

Define the study assistant's purpose and design a basic probabilistic model to handle uncertainty in learning behavior or performance.

**Your tasks**

- 
Define the use case
Describe what your study assistant is designed to do. Examples:

Recommend which topic to study next.

- 
Predict quiz success based on past performance.

- 
Suggest optimal study times.

- 
Remind students of topics likely to be forgotten.

- 
List key variables or events
Identify at least 2-3 measurable variables or outcomes your assistant will monitor (e.g., quiz score, time since last review, topic accuracy).

- 
Build a simple probability model
Using what you have learned:

Define relevant probabilities or conditional probabilities.

- 
Optionally apply Bayes’ Theorem to update beliefs about the student’s knowledge level.

- Explain in plain language what these probabilities mean in your context.

- Brief reflection
In a few sentences, explain how probability helps your study assistant make smarter decisions and adapt to uncertain or changing inputs.
